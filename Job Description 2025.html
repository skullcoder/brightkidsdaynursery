JOB DESCRIPTION for Deputy Manager - Bright Kids Day Nursery

Main Responsibilities

  • To deputise for the Manager
  • To ensure a high standard of physical, emotional, social and intellectual care and development for children placed in the Day Nursery.
  • To be responsible for any tasks delegated by the Manager.
  • To give support to other personnel within the Day Nursery.

Main Duties

Overall management responsibility for the Day Nursery in the absence of the Manager.

Assist the Manager in providing effective day to day management of the Nursery.

  • Assist the Manager in setting and implementing objectives for policy for the Nursery within the framework of the EYFS.
  • Assist the Manager in planning the training strategy for students and staff.
  • To be responsible for the supervision of students, identifying their learning needs and advising key-staff of these.
  • In conjunction with the Manager, to formulate, operate and evaluate a yearly programme of activities, following the guidelines of the National Curriculum, which is suitable for the age range of children.
  • To assist the Manager in promoting Equal Opportunities throughout the Day Nursery.
  • To work alongside the parents/carers of special needs children to achieve full integration within the Nursery.
  • Identify the physical, emotional, intellectual and developmental needs of children with special needs and offer support and relevant developmental opportunities.
  • Liaise with and support parents/carers and other family members.
  • Liaise with the Local authority and other professionals associated with the Nursery.
  • Attend monthly staff meetings and training sessions outside working hours.
  • Undertake certain domestic duties within the Nursery, i.e, preparation of snacks, cleansing of equipment.
  • Work alongside the Manager and staff team to ensure that the philosophy behind the Nursery is fulfilled.
  • Undertake other duties as may be requested from time to time by the Manager.
  • To plan 'Open Day' for the nursery to promoting it.

PERSON SPECIFICATION for Deputy Manager

Experience

  • 3 years post-qualifying experience in a Day Care Setting
  • Experience in a Senior Position
  • Experience of working with children aged 0 - 5 years

Education / Training Qualifications

  • NVQ Level 3 or above Early Years Education and Childcare qualification
  • NNEB
  • Other acceptable qualifications (as stated in the QCA guidelines)
  • A recent First Aid qualification
  • Basic Food and Hygiene Certificate

Specialist Knowledge

  • Knowledge of The Children Act
  • Knowledge of the EYFS 2017 and 2025, including Development Matters
  • Knowledge of Birth to Three Matters
  • Knowledge Statutory Framework for the EYFS 2017 and 2025
  • The ability to demonstrate an up to date knowledge of current thinking and development in early years
  • Awareness of planning/record keeping and Key Worker systems
  • Awareness of Health and Safety and Risk Assessments
  • Safeguarding & Child Protection, including 'Whistleblowing' and what to do if an allegation is made against a member of staff including the Manager or the Owner of the nursery.
  • How to do children's 'Learning Journals'

Skills and Abilities

  • An in depth understanding of Equal Opportunities
  • Ability to communicate confidently with a wide range of people
  • Good organisational skills
  • Ability to prioritise work and delegate appropriately
  • To be able to feedback information in the form of written reports to the Employer as requested.
  • To support the Nursery Manager and Staff Team
  • To be able to manage the nursery in the absence of the Nursery Manager
  • To be able to promote the nursery in a positive and professional manner
  • An understanding of children's needs and behaviour
  • The ability to set appropriate limits for children

Marketing & Customer Care

  • Proactively represent the company within the local community professionally.
  • Promote the nursery to current parents and potential customers
  • Ensure all staff develop and maintain friendly professional relationship with parents and carers.
  • Assists the Nursery manager in ensuring that all complaints and concerns are actively resolved in a timely manner and are reported to the owner.

General

  • Assists the Nursery Manager/Employer with administrative duties associated with the management of the nursery such as maintaining records on children and families maintaining inventories and keeping in line personnel records.
  • Adhere to the company policies and procedures and review them as necessary.
  • Undertake any other duties as reasonably requested by the line manager and employer
  • Maintain effective methods of communication with children, staff, parents, carers and attend meetings as and when required.

Finance

  • Assists the Nursery Manager in Managing staff costs, petty cash and other budgets and maintaining petty cash systems.
  • Assists the nursery manager with the collection and recording of fees.
  • Report any concerns of any staff and Manager directly to the employer.
  • Report any mismanagement of finance directly to the employer
  • Learn fees systems and implement it effectively and report of any fee that is outstanding to the Nursery Manager and Employer.
  • Report of any money that goes missing from the nursery directly to the Employer.

Health & Safety

  • Support the Nursery Manager in the day to day operations in order to ensure the health and safety of the children their parents carers the team and any visitors to the nursery.
  • Adhere to all health & safety policies and procedures
  • Be fully aware of all emergency and security procedures
  • Be responsible for ensuring the nursery remains compliant in respect of suitable trained staff with relevant first aid qualification.
  • To ensure risk assessments are carried out in and around the building.

Operational

  • Assists the Nursery Manager with the effective day to day management of the nursery
  • Assists the Nursery Manager with all the day to day paper work within the nursery
  • Support the Nursery Manager and staff during inspections by the regulatory bodies and assists in the implementation of any recommendations.
  • Assists the nursery manager and employer in delivering agreed occupancy targets keep the nursery presentable at all times.
  • Assists the Nursery Manager in promoting the nursery through Open days, Coffee Mornings etc.

Staff

  • Assists the Nursery Manager in the recruitment and induction of all new staff
  • Support, supervise, train and praise all staff to ensure delivery of high-quality childcare practice.
  • Support and supervise staff with planning, room meetings and writing of reports.
  • Support the Nursery Manager to identify training needs, develop training plans and evaluate training undertaken by staff.
  • Work in conjunction with the employer in disciplinary and grievance investigations and hearings.
  • Develop an open and positive relationship with staff.

The duties and responsibilities in this job description are not exhaustive or restrictive; changes relevant to this post may be added. The job description may be reviewed in the future.

JOB DESCRIPTION for Nursery Practitioners - Bright Kids Day Nursery

The Nursery Practitioners will be working as Key Workers for the daily needs of children between the ages of birth and five years within the Nursery setting

Qualities and Experience Required

NVQ Level 3, NNEB or equivalent qualification, with experience in working within the setting of a Day Nursery. You will need to be flexible regarding hours of work and motivated.

Qualifications will be checked using the DEF early years qualification checklist. Check an early years qualification - GOV.UK

  • Basic Food Hygiene Certificate (Essential)
  • Paediatric First Aid (Essential)

Purpose of Post

  • To ensure a high standard of physical, emotional, social and intellectual care for children placed in the Day Nursery
  • To give support to other personnel within the Day Nursery
  • To implement the daily routine in the base room.
  • To implement EYFS
  • To implement children's 'Learning Journals'

Key Areas

  • Work with children
  • Team work
  • Liaise with Parents, Staff and other Professionals
  • Have a good understanding of how children develop and learn according to the age and stages.

Responsible to:

Room Leader, Nursery Manager/ Deputy Manager

Duties and Responsibilities

  • Formulate and operate a programme of activities suitable to the age range of children attending the Nursery in conjunction with the Manager.
  • Prepare the children's 'Learning Journals' in your group and prepare reviews for parents, in conjunction with the Manager.
  • Work alongside parents of special needs children and give physical, emotional, and intellectual guidance as appropriate.
  • Foresee the needs of special needs children and give physical, emotional, and intellectual guidance as appropriate and liaise with the settings SENCO.
  • Support all staff and engage in a good staff team.
  • Liaise with and support parents and other family members.
  • Attend staff meetings outside working hours
  • Undertake certain domestic jobs within the Nursery, for example, preparation of snack meals, cleansing of equipment.
  • To attend out of working hours activities, e.g. training, staff meetings, parent's evenings, summer fate and Christmas activities.
  • To be flexible within working practises within the setting be prepared to work where needed and undertaking certain domestic tasks, e.g snacks
  • Monitor all accidents/incidents in the room and record them, check to see if they have been signed by the parents, manager and staff.
  • Ensure children are collected by someone known to the setting
  • To respect the confidentiality of information received
  • To develop your role within the team
  • Participate in the training programme of a wide variety of students (i.e, placements and volunteers) by giving guidance and support.
  • Work alongside the Manager and staff team to ensure that the philosophy behind the project is fulfilled.
  • Plan and set up activities that are stimulating and age appropriate, this also includes display boards.

The duties and responsibilities in this job description are not exhaustive or restrictive; changes relevant to this post may be added. The job description may be reviewed in the future. Willingness to undertake additional training is necessary.

Bright Kids Day Nursery - Day Nursery Cook Job Description

Job – Part-time

Education/training - Food & Hygiene

Key Responsibilities

  • To organise, cook, and prepare a healthy, balanced diet for children aged 0 to 5 years in accordance with the nursery menus.
  • Take into consideration daily those children who have special dietary requirements and prepare a suitable alternative.
  • Prepare a suitably balanced rotating menu taking into account dietary and cultural requirements of the children.
  • Ensure high standards of hygiene, health and safety is maintained in the kitchen at all times.

AREAS OF RESPONSIBILITY

  • To be responsible for the day-to-day running of the kitchen, ensuring compliance with registration and legislative requirements at all times.
  • Responsible for the planning and preparation of food to provide meal
  • Ensure that all individual dietary requirements are catered for in line with parental guidance.
  • Ensure daily and weekly checks are filled out and tasks are completed.
  • Maintain necessary kitchen procedures and records as required by Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd and Environmental Health policies.
  • Liaise with nursery Manager on the ordering of necessary kitchen equipment.
  • To undertake any other reasonable catering duties as requested by the Nursery Manager.
  • Ensure the kitchen and equipment is kept suitably clean at all times including walls, floors and trolleys to transport food following a cleaning schedule. On a daily basis that all crockery, cutlery and cooking utensils are washed in accordance with food safety guidelines.
  • Ensure that all foods are ready at the specified time and correctly served.
  • To implement and maintain the company's equal opportunities policy ensuring that children, parents and the staff are valued.
  • To monitor any safeguarding issues following local authority procedures.
  • Ensure food is cut appropriately to minimise any choking risks. This includes meat such as sausages, fruit and vegetables.
  • Ensure the effective and regular removal of waste materials to the designated waste area.
  • The ability to use food preparation equipment and ensure the economic use of materials, equipment and energy.
  • To have total responsibility of the kitchen, cleaning all of the equipment and appliances, taking temperatures of the food, fridge and freezer and recording these in the kitchen file.
  • To work as a team and help out in the nursery when needed.
  • Responsible for control, storage and rotation of kitchen stock.
  • The ability to order correct amounts of food and control kitchen stock.
  • To contribute to the team effort of the nursery and to attend staff meetings and social events as and when required.
  • Commitment to undertaking appropriate training and continued professional development.
  • To undertake future and refresher training as appropriate.
  • To ensure that the highest standards of health and safety apply to both children, staff, parents and visitors, maintaining a safe and secure environment.
  • Ensure that hygiene and cleanliness within the kitchen are of the highest standard in accordance with Environmental Health requirements and legislation.
  • Maintain an awareness of food allergies keeping an up to date log of all children in the nursery, working with the staff team during new admissions.
  • Update your knowledge and awareness of Health and Safety issues including COSHH, regularly.
  • To be responsible for the hygiene and complete all Risk assessments within the kitchen area.
  • Prevent cross-contamination of food within the kitchen area.
  • Temperatures of high-risk food must be recorded at the time of delivery and cooking. All food must be stored and served at the temperatures required by the Chartered Institute of Environmental Health-Food Safety.
  • To work across both units as required by the Manager.

The duties and responsibilities in this job description are not exhaustive or restrictive; changes relevant to this post may be added. The job description may be reviewed in the future.

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd - Nursery Cleaner Job Description

Job - Parttime

Education/training - none but experience required

Main Duties

To implement and promote all Bright Kids Policies and the Staff Rules.

To ensure their safety and welfare of the children at all times.

To carry out light physical duties required during housekeeping.

To be responsible for maintaining the highest level of hygiene and cleanliness throughout the nursery premises.

This is achieved by:

  • ensuring the daily thorough cleaning of all nursery rooms, including floor coverings, sink, cobwebs removed, areas, walls and paintwork, windows etc, (Hoovering, mopping and wiping down)
  • ensuring the daily thorough cleaning of all nursery furniture and equipment
  • ensuring the daily thorough cleaning of the nursery kitchen floor.
  • ensuring the daily thorough cleaning of child and staff toilet areas
  • ensuring the daily thorough cleaning of other nursery areas, including corridors, hallways, the Staff Room and all other areas of the nursery.
  • Moving the furniture once a week to clean behind it. This includes staff room furniture.
  • To ensure that the inside of the windows and sills are cleaned weekly.
  • To ensure the garden is swept weekly
  • To put toilet rolls in every toilet
  • Remove bins and replace bin bags
  • Keep record, maintaining sufficient supplies of domestic consumables eg, toilet rolls, liquid soap, cleaning materials, etc and report it to the manager to replace detergents.
  • Complete the daily cleaning records and report any damages or shortages of supplies needed to complete your role. Report of anything that is of concern, this includes mice droppings and broken items.
