Policy Statement

This policy outlines the procedures for managing staff and child absences to ensure continuity of care and maintain high standards of service delivery.

Staff Absence Procedures

Reporting Absence

  • Staff must report absence as soon as possible, preferably before the start of their shift
  • Contact the manager directly on [phone number]
  • Provide reason for absence and expected duration
  • Follow up with written confirmation within 24 hours

Cover Arrangements

  • Manager will arrange appropriate cover for absent staff
  • Maintain required staff-to-child ratios at all times
  • Use qualified agency staff when necessary
  • Document all cover arrangements

Child Absence Procedures

Parent Notification

  • Parents must notify the nursery of child absence by 9:00 AM
  • Provide reason for absence
  • Inform of expected return date

Record Keeping

  • Record all absences in the attendance register
  • Note reason for absence
  • Monitor patterns of absence
  • Follow up on unexplained absences

Behaviour Management Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy provides guidelines for dealing with challenging behaviour in children, including the use of behaviour and biting logs to monitor and address issues effectively.

Understanding Challenging Behaviour

Challenging behaviour is any behaviour that interferes with a child's learning, development, or the safety of themselves or others. This may include:

  • Aggressive behaviour (hitting, biting, kicking)
  • Disruptive behaviour (screaming, throwing objects)
  • Withdrawn behaviour (refusing to participate, isolating themselves)
  • Destructive behaviour (breaking toys, damaging property)

Positive Behaviour Management Strategies

Prevention

  • Create a positive, stimulating environment
  • Establish clear, consistent routines
  • Provide appropriate activities for each child's developmental stage
  • Ensure adequate supervision and support

Intervention

  • Use positive reinforcement and praise
  • Redirect attention to appropriate activities
  • Set clear, age-appropriate boundaries
  • Use calm, consistent responses

Recording and Monitoring

Behaviour Logs

  • Record all incidents of challenging behaviour
  • Include date, time, location, and circumstances
  • Note triggers and responses
  • Monitor patterns and frequency

Biting Logs

  • Document all biting incidents immediately
  • Record both biter and victim details
  • Note context and circumstances
  • Implement appropriate interventions

Child Protection Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

We are committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all children in our care. This policy outlines our procedures for protecting children from harm and ensuring their safety and well-being.

Legal Framework

This policy is based on:

  • Children Act 1989 and 2004
  • Working Together to Safeguard Children 2018
  • Keeping Children Safe in Education 2021
  • Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) 2021

Types of Abuse

Physical Abuse

Physical harm to a child, including hitting, shaking, burning, or any other form of physical injury.

Emotional Abuse

Persistent emotional maltreatment that affects a child's emotional development, including humiliation, rejection, or isolation.

Sexual Abuse

Any sexual activity with a child, including inappropriate touching, exposure to sexual content, or sexual exploitation.

Neglect

Failure to meet a child's basic physical and emotional needs, including inadequate supervision, nutrition, or medical care.

Procedures

Recognising Concerns

  • All staff are trained to recognise signs of abuse
  • Concerns must be reported immediately to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)
  • Document all concerns accurately and confidentially
  • Follow the "What to do if you're worried a child is being abused" guidance

Reporting Procedures

  • Immediate reporting to DSL or deputy DSL
  • Contact local authority children's services if necessary
  • Refer to police if there is immediate danger
  • Maintain confidentiality and follow data protection guidelines

Fire Safety Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy ensures the safety of all children, staff, and visitors in the event of a fire emergency. We are committed to maintaining a safe environment and providing clear procedures for fire prevention and evacuation.

Fire Prevention

Regular Checks

  • Daily checks of fire exits and escape routes
  • Weekly testing of fire alarms and emergency lighting
  • Monthly inspection of fire extinguishers
  • Annual professional fire safety assessment

Safety Measures

  • Keep all fire exits clear and unobstructed
  • Maintain working fire detection systems
  • Store flammable materials safely
  • Ensure all electrical equipment is PAT tested

Evacuation Procedures

On Discovery of Fire

  • Raise the alarm immediately
  • Call 999 for fire service
  • Evacuate all children and staff to assembly point
  • Do not attempt to fight the fire unless trained

Evacuation Process

  • Follow designated escape routes
  • Take children to assembly point
  • Conduct roll call to ensure everyone is accounted for
  • Wait for fire service to arrive

Fire Drills

  • Conduct fire drills monthly
  • Test different scenarios and escape routes
  • Ensure all staff are familiar with procedures
  • Record all drills and review performance

Mobile Phone Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy outlines the appropriate use of mobile phones and electronic devices within the nursery setting to ensure the safety and well-being of children and maintain professional standards.

Staff Use of Mobile Phones

During Working Hours

  • Personal mobile phones must be switched off or on silent during working hours
  • Phones should be stored in lockers or designated areas
  • Emergency calls only during working hours
  • No personal use of phones in areas where children are present

Photography and Recording

  • No personal photography or recording of children
  • Use only nursery-approved devices for official photos
  • Follow data protection and safeguarding guidelines
  • Obtain proper consent for any photography

Parent and Visitor Use

  • Parents and visitors should limit mobile phone use while on premises
  • No photography or recording without permission
  • Respect the privacy of other children and families
  • Follow nursery guidelines for electronic device use

Emergency Procedures

  • Nursery phones available for emergency contact
  • Staff may use personal phones for genuine emergencies only
  • Report any emergency phone use to management
  • Maintain professional standards at all times

Coronavirus Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy outlines our approach to managing coronavirus (COVID-19) risks within the nursery setting, ensuring the safety and well-being of all children, staff, and families while maintaining high-quality early years provision.

Prevention Measures

Hygiene Practices

  • Regular hand washing with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  • Use of hand sanitizer when hand washing is not possible
  • Enhanced cleaning of surfaces and equipment
  • Proper respiratory hygiene (catch it, bin it, kill it)

Social Distancing

  • Maintain appropriate distancing where possible
  • Limit mixing between different groups
  • Staggered drop-off and collection times
  • Outdoor activities where appropriate

Symptom Management

Staff and Children

  • Stay home if experiencing COVID-19 symptoms
  • Follow government guidance on isolation periods
  • Seek medical advice if symptoms persist
  • Return only when fully recovered and no longer infectious

Reporting

  • Report positive cases to relevant authorities
  • Inform parents of any confirmed cases
  • Follow contact tracing procedures
  • Maintain confidentiality of personal information

Adaptations

  • Modified activities to reduce transmission risk
  • Enhanced ventilation in indoor spaces
  • Regular review and update of procedures
  • Staff training on current guidance

Reporting Concerns - Safeguarding Procedures

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy provides clear procedures for reporting safeguarding concerns to ensure the safety and well-being of all children in our care. All staff have a duty to report any concerns about a child's welfare.

Types of Concerns

Immediate Concerns

  • Physical injury or signs of abuse
  • Disclosure of abuse by a child
  • Inappropriate behaviour by adults
  • Any situation where a child is at immediate risk

General Concerns

  • Changes in a child's behaviour or appearance
  • Unexplained injuries or marks
  • Inappropriate comments or drawings
  • Concerns about a child's home environment

Reporting Procedures

Immediate Action

  • Report concerns immediately to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)
  • If DSL is unavailable, contact deputy DSL or manager
  • Document all concerns accurately and confidentially
  • Follow up with written report within 24 hours

External Reporting

  • Contact local authority children's services if necessary
  • Refer to police if there is immediate danger
  • Follow "What to do if you're worried a child is being abused" guidance
  • Maintain confidentiality and data protection standards

Documentation

  • Record all concerns in safeguarding log
  • Include date, time, and details of concern
  • Note actions taken and outcomes
  • Store securely and maintain confidentiality

Nappy Changing Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Policy Statement

This policy ensures that nappy changing is carried out safely, hygienically, and respectfully, promoting the child's dignity and well-being while maintaining high standards of hygiene and infection control.

Hygiene Standards

Hand Hygiene

  • Wash hands thoroughly before and after each nappy change
  • Use disposable gloves for each change
  • Dispose of gloves immediately after use
  • Use hand sanitizer if hand washing is not immediately available

Changing Area

  • Clean and disinfect changing mat before each use
  • Ensure adequate ventilation in changing area
  • Keep changing area well-stocked with supplies
  • Dispose of waste in appropriate bins

Changing Procedure

Preparation

  • Gather all necessary supplies before starting
  • Ensure changing area is clean and ready
  • Wash hands and put on disposable gloves
  • Ensure child is comfortable and secure

During Change

  • Talk to the child throughout the process
  • Clean thoroughly from front to back
  • Use appropriate cleaning products
  • Apply barrier cream if needed

After Change

  • Dispose of nappy and gloves immediately
  • Wash hands thoroughly
  • Clean and disinfect changing area
  • Record change in nappy changing log

Record Keeping

  • Record each nappy change with time and details
  • Note any concerns about child's health or skin condition
  • Share information with parents as appropriate
  • Maintain confidentiality of records
