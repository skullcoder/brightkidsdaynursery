Induction Procedure

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Purpose

The induction procedure ensures that all new staff members receive comprehensive training and orientation to enable them to perform their roles effectively and safely within our nursery environment.

Pre-Employment Requirements

Documentation

  • DBS check (Disclosure and Barring Service)
  • References from previous employers
  • Qualification certificates and training records
  • Medical clearance and health declaration
  • Right to work documentation

Initial Assessment

  • Skills and experience evaluation
  • Training needs identification
  • Role-specific requirements assessment
  • Personal development planning

Induction Program

Week 1: Foundation

  • Nursery tour and facility orientation
  • Introduction to team members and management
  • Health and safety briefing
  • Child protection and safeguarding overview
  • Basic policies and procedures introduction

Week 2-4: Core Training

  • Detailed policy training sessions
  • Practical skills development
  • Shadowing experienced staff members
  • Observation and feedback sessions
  • Assessment of competency levels

Ongoing Support

  • Regular check-ins with line manager
  • Additional training as required
  • Performance reviews and feedback
  • Professional development opportunities

Assessment and Sign-off

  • Competency assessment after 4 weeks
  • Policy knowledge test
  • Practical skills evaluation
  • Line manager sign-off for independent working
  • Probationary period completion

Hours and Breaks Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Working Hours

Standard Working Hours

  • Full-time staff: 40 hours per week
  • Part-time staff: As per contract agreement
  • Shift patterns: (7:45 AM - 5:45 PM), (08:00 AM - 05:00 PM),(8:30 AM - 05:30 PM), (09:00 AM to 6:00 PM)

Flexible Working

  • Flexible start and finish times within core hours
  • Compressed hours arrangements
  • Job sharing opportunities
  • Remote working for administrative tasks where appropriate
  • Flexible working is a request and must be put in writing.

Break Entitlements

Lunch Breaks

  • One hour lunch break away from the children.

Attendance and Punctuality

  • Arrive 15 minutes before shift start for handover
  • Clock in/out system for accurate time recording
  • Notification of lateness or absence as soon as possible
  • Return to work interviews after sickness absence
  • Disciplinary procedures for persistent lateness

Holiday Entitlement and Application

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Holiday Entitlement

Annual Leave Allowance

  • Full-time staff: 28 days per year (including bank holidays)
  • Part-time staff: Pro-rata entitlement

Bank Holidays

  • 8 bank holidays per year included in entitlement
  • Christmas and New Year arrangements

Holiday Application Process

Request Procedure

  • Submit holiday request form at least 4 weeks in advance
  • Peak periods require 8 weeks notice
  • Maximum 2 weeks consecutive leave during term time
  • Line manager approval required

Peak Periods and Restrictions

  • Summer holidays: Limited availability
  • Christmas period: No Holiday during this period
  • Half-term breaks: First come, first served basis
  • Training days: No holiday requests accepted
  • Religious Holiday- A request and approval from the Manager is required.

Emergency Leave and Special Circumstances

Emergency Leave

  • Bereavement leave: Up to 5 days paid leave
  • Emergency family situations: Unpaid leave
  • Medical appointments: Time off as required
  • Jury service: Paid leave with documentation
  • Bereavement leave: Up to 5 days paid leave. Only immediate family members refer to the Staff Handbook.
  • Medical appointments: Time off as required with supporting document

Maternity/Paternity Leave

  • Statutory maternity leave: 52 weeks
  • Maternity pay: As per statutory requirements
  • Paternity leave: 2 weeks paid leave
  • Shared parental leave: As per legislation

Holiday Management

  • Holiday calendar maintained by management
  • Staff portal for holiday requests and balances
  • Regular updates on remaining entitlement
  • End-of-year holiday reconciliation

Compulsory Monthly Staff Meetings

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Meeting Requirements

All staff members are required to attend monthly staff meetings as part of their employment contract. These meetings are essential for maintaining communication, sharing updates, and ensuring consistent practice across the nursery.

Meeting Schedule

Regular Schedule

  • Every 2 months or earlier at 6.00pm
  • Duration: 1-2 hours depending on agenda
  • Alternative dates for bank holidays

Special Meetings

  • Emergency meetings as required
  • Training sessions and workshops
  • Policy update meetings
  • Team building activities

Meeting Content

Regular Agenda Items

  • Previous month's performance review
  • Policy updates and changes
  • Training announcements and requirements
  • Health and safety updates
  • Staff feedback and suggestions

Additional Topics

  • New staff introductions
  • Parent feedback and concerns
  • Regulatory updates and compliance
  • Upcoming events and activities
  • Resource and equipment updates

Attendance Requirements

Mandatory Attendance

  • All full-time and part-time staff must attend
  • Absence requires prior approval from line manager
  • Medical certificates required for illness absence
  • Make-up sessions for missed meetings

Documentation

  • Attendance register maintained
  • Minutes recorded and distributed
  • Action points assigned and tracked
  • Follow-up on previous meeting items

Consequences of Non-Attendance

  • First absence: Verbal warning and catch-up session
  • Repeated absences: Written warning
  • Persistent non-attendance: Disciplinary action
  • Impact on performance reviews and promotions

Organizational Structure

Management Hierarchy

  • Nursery Manager: Overall responsibility and strategic direction in charge of the nursery under OFSTED registration.
  • Deputy Manager: Operational management/supporting the Manager and staff
  • Senior Practitioners: Advanced practice and mentoring
  • Practitioners: Core childcare delivery
  • Support Staff: Apprentices new to their role, learning and mentoring qualified staff

Reporting Structure

  • Practitioners report to Senior Leaders
  • Senior Leaders report to Deputy Manager
  • Deputy Manager reports to Nursery Manager
  • Nursery Manager reports to Directors

Team Senior Leader Role

Key Responsibilities

  • Daily room management and staff coordination
  • Staff supervision and performance management
  • Parent communication and relationship building
  • Curriculum planning and implementation
  • Health and safety compliance in assigned areas

Leadership Qualities

  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills
  • Experience in early years education
  • Leadership and team management abilities
  • Problem-solving and decision-making skills
  • Commitment to continuous professional development

Staff Development and Progression

Career Pathways

  • Senior Practitioner to Team Leader promotion
  • Team Leader to Deputy Manager advancement
  • Specialist roles and additional responsibilities

Performance Management

  • Regular one-to-one meetings with line managers
  • Annual performance reviews and appraisals
  • Personal development planning
  • Training needs identification and support

Communication and Support

  • Open-door policy for staff concerns
  • Regular team meetings and briefings

WhatsApp Groups Policy

Bright Kids Day Nursery Ltd / Bright Kids Hackney Ltd
Adopted: [Date] | To be reviewed: [Date]

Purpose and Guidelines

WhatsApp groups are used for efficient communication and coordination among staff members. This policy ensures professional and appropriate use of these communication channels while maintaining confidentiality and respect.

Group Structure

Official Groups

  • All Staff Group: General announcements and updates
  • Management Group: Leadership communication
  • Team Leader Group: Operational coordination
  • Room Groups: Specific room-based communication
  • Training Group: Professional development updates

Group Administration

  • Group admins: Management and Team Leaders only
  • Member approval required for new additions
  • Regular review of group membership
  • Clear group descriptions and purposes

Communication Standards

Appropriate Content

  • Work-related announcements and updates
  • Shift changes and cover requests
  • Training reminders and professional development
  • Team building and social events
  • Emergency communications and urgent matters

Inappropriate Content

  • Personal conversations unrelated to work
  • Confidential information about children or families
  • Inappropriate language or content
  • Gossip or negative comments about colleagues
  • Political or controversial discussions

Professional Conduct

Communication Etiquette

  • Use professional language and tone
  • Be respectful and considerate of others
  • Keep messages concise and relevant
  • Use appropriate timing for non-urgent messages
  • Respond promptly to work-related queries

Privacy and Confidentiality

  • Never share confidential information
  • Respect colleagues' privacy
  • Do not screenshot or forward messages inappropriately
  • Report any breaches of confidentiality

Monitoring and Compliance

  • Regular monitoring of group content
  • Addressing inappropriate behavior promptly
  • Training on appropriate use of communication tools
  • Disciplinary action for policy violations
  • Regular review and update of group guidelines
